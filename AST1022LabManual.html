Astronomy 1022 Lab Manual Changes

During the spring semester of 2022, I was the instructor for the University of Florida Astronomy 1022L lab course. During this time, I became increasing frustrated with the quality and age of the lab manual we had students using. For context, most of the lab manual was originally written before I was born... and I was teaching the course. Needless to say, it needed an upgrade.


I took it upon myself to copy over the lab manual and make improvements as I went. This task started simple: some figures had been photocopied over and over and over... and over, to the point where the quality of the image suffered. This evolved into a whole new refresh of the entire formatting of the textbook into LaTeX (not to mention ALEX labs -- which can be seen here)


One quality of the updated tenth edition of "Hands On Astronomy" that I am particularly proud of is the addition of vectorized images for virtually all of the figures in the manual via a LaTeX package named Tikz. I show some particularly good examples a little later.

Changes

For a reference (and for my own personal pride), I have compiled visual exmaples of a couple of the biggest changes here. In addition, here's a nice summary of all the work that I did for the tenth edition of the lab:

  • Transferred the entire document from Microsoft Word to LaTeX (via Overleaf)
    • Individual labs can be edited separately and compiled together into one lightweight (3.2 MB) pdf file
  • Found high quality pdfs/pngs of all existing photographs/historical references in the lab manual
  • Recreated any figure that did not fall into the category of photograph or historical reference using Tikz (over 25 total diagrams, see below for examples)
    • In addition to this, I created new figures to help with the clarity of the manual
  • Created an automatic table of contents and numbering system that updates universally with minimal effort
  • Reworked the computer software for three labs (ALEX)
    • This included rewriting large chunks of text in the manual as well as reworking the conceptual elements of the labs
  • Clarified directions by editing text in nearly every single experiment
  • Replaced he/she pronouns in reference to the instructor with they/them
  • Unified the style of the entire manual
