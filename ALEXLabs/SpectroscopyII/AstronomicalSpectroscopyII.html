Identifying Spectral Lines

Before we get into the nitty-gritty of why the Sun does what it does, let's take a step back and see what it does. In this experiment, you will see a mock stellar spectrum. You will notice that there are several wavelengths missing from this spectrum. As you learned in Astronomical Spectroscopy I, one of Kirchoff's Laws tells us about absorption spectra. The radiation from the Sun is one example of a place that we see these absorption lines in a continuous source.

The most conspicuous lines are called "Fraunhofer Lines" (named after the man who discovered and catalogued them). As Kirchoff showed, each of these lines corresponds to a different electron transition within atoms. Use the table in your lab manual and see if you can identify which line is which Fraunhofer line, as well as what element it corresponds to.
